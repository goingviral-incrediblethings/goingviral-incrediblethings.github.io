  • In the morning after the storm...

    by Beth Timson

  • I want to hold you, but I can’t

    by Beth Timson


In the morning after the storm I will be waiting to give love to strangers,
On streets and neighbours, I did not know and when we meet again it will be
Like the first time, like we never met before,
Let's relive our youths once taken from us,
In ecstasy of not knowing what mistakes will make
But that this thing called life is ours for however long we have it
And if it is to be lived it is to be with love, courage and eagerness
Nothing is fixed or like it was and now is our opportunity to demand more.
More from the owners of property they wish to not share
More from each other when we are alone
For we all know trauma, and it’s shaking of our safety
Means fear is pointless for we have it all to gain
Life at its fullest
In its most colourful capacity
In which each version of humanity can exist as effervescently as they deserve
For life was almost lost but was it being lived before?
