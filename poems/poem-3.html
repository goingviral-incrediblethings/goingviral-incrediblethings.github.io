I want to hold you, but I can’t

by Beth Timson

I want to hold you, but I can’t
I don’t think I ever have not really
I don’t think we could
Cause when we learnt to hold ourselves before anyone else touched us
It's hard to be seen if your truth has so many layers
To pull away
I know whispers you aren't okay, and you know mine
But the world says we are clever and articulate
And we stand up and out and, in our truth,
But what if we never chose to stand out and the
Words we share so articulately aren't enough
Language to reach each other because language
Is only a mask the wise use to pretend we aren’t just like you
If you saw her the mornings she didn’t get up or
When fear encompassed maybe you’d know that
This is no power, but weakness shrouded by a false sense of
Security.
We make you feel safe to accommodate for the times we never did I hear whispers on phone calls that you aren’t okay
Fall for a bit but only to allow grazed knees
I can’t hold you right now
